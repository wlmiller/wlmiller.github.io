About me

I’m a software engineer based in Houston, TX. I earned a Ph.D. from Columbia University studying self-assembly in soft matter systems. In particular, I wrote, ran, and analyzed simulations of such systems; you can see a couple of examples of such simulations on GitHub.

Currently, I'm working as a Technology Architect at Durwella. Durwella is a software solutions company that focuses on scientific and engineering projects; I use my technical expertise and scientific background to develop a variety of applications. My primary focus is cross-platform mobile applications using frameworks such as Xamarin and Ionic, but I’m not limited to such projects – for example, recent work has involved extensions for the Petrel and SKUA-GOCAD geoscience platforms, with other wide-ranging projects planned.
